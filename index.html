About Us

Mandi Litster

Mandi Litster, born 2000 in Utah, is a child at heart. She's a passionate character artist, enjoys a good story, and has frequent cravings to solve puzzles of all sorts (especially chess!). She also loves video games, and you can often find her playing Bejeweled, Minecraft, Synth Riders VR, and Tetris--LOTS of Tetris! Mandi has a special place in her heart for cats (but she likes dogs, too!).

Noah Heber

Hello! I'm Noah. I was born in 2003 in Utah, and I've lived here almost all my life. I'm a software developer, and I love creating solutions to complex problems. I also love gaming, with Minecraft, Starcraft 2, and Space Engineers being my top favorite. I also like camping, cleaning, and telling Mandi she's the cutest ❤. I'm also an ordinance worker at the Draper, Utah temple, and there's no place on Earth I'd rather be.

Timeline

So it begins...
April 22nd, 2019

They first met on an LDS-focused online gaming community, when Mandi gave feedback on a new hymn idea Noah was writing. Over the years, they played games like Minecraft together, and took part in community events.

First Meeting
July 15th, 2023

They met in person for the first time in front of the Draper, Utah temple. Mandi had begun a regular trip to the temple with her friends and had a prompting from the Spirit to invite Noah.

A first date planned...
July 23rd, 2023

Noah, with much anxiety, invited Mandi to a date. As it turns out, she was planning on asking him out too, so it worked out perfectly!

First Date
July 27th, 2023

Mandi & Noah go on their first date - Leatherby's to eat ice cream, and then a movie night, watching Ant-Man and the Wasp: Quantumania.

Love
March 10th, 2024

After much temple time and discussion, Mandi and Noah decide they want to get married. Noah gets her father's blessing.

Engagement
March 13th, 2024

In front of the Jordan River temple, and under the guise of a "scripture study session," Noah proposes to Mandi, and they become engaged!

Wedding
June 15th, 2024

Mandi and Noah will be married and sealed for time and all eternity on June 15th, 2024, in the Jordan River, Utah temple.
